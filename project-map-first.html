Rome2rio · 2025 · Concept

Map First
Concept

A reimagining of the rome2rio mobile app: what if the map was the product, not the result? A spatial exploration model that puts understanding before routing.

Product Concept Mobile UX Strategy Interaction Design

My role

Design lead and concept author

Type

Strategic concept / speculative design

Status

Concept — in exploration

The insight

Rome2rio is built on a list. You enter an origin and destination, and you get a list of routes. It's functional. It works. But it assumes you already know where you're going and exactly how you want to frame the journey.

Travel research doesn't work that way. People browse. They wonder. "Could I take the train?" "What's between here and there?" "Is there a ferry?" The list model answers the question you've already decided to ask — Map First answers the questions you haven't formed yet.

The concept

Map First places the map as the primary surface of the rome2rio mobile app. Instead of typing a destination into a search box and receiving a list of routes, the traveller opens the app to a map of the world and starts exploring spatially.

Tap a city. See what routes connect to where you are. Pan to a region you've been curious about. Discover that there's a direct overnight train you didn't know existed. The product shifts from a query-response model to an exploratory, browsable one.

"People don't just want to get somewhere. They want to understand where they're going — and what's possible."

Design principles

1
Spatial first, list second The map is the entry point, not the result page. Lists appear contextually as you interact with destinations — never as the first thing you see.
2
Progressive detail The map starts simple and surfaces detail on demand. Tapping a route shows mode, duration, cost. Tapping again opens the full itinerary. Depth is available — it's just not forced.
3
Discovery as a feature Serendipitous connections — routes you didn't search for — are surfaced based on proximity and context. Exploration is rewarded.

Why this matters strategically

Most travel apps compete on the same query-response model. Map First is a differentiated bet: that the most engaging travel product isn't the one that answers your question fastest, but the one that expands what questions you think to ask.

This positions rome2rio not just as a routing utility but as the place where travel thinking starts.
