LoyaltyCorp · 2016–2019

eWallet

LoyaltyCorp is transforming how consumers engage and transact with loyalty programs through its eWallet solution. Embedded across all LoyaltyCorp platforms, eWallet gives consumers one place to access purchases, points, bank accounts, and funds, bridging brand loyalty with seamless payments. I led the end-to-end design of the consumer-facing widget and payment flows.

Fintech White-label Payments 2FA

My role

Lead product designer

Team

Engineering, PM, client stakeholders

Scope

Widget, payments, auth flows

The challenge

Loyalty program members had no single place to manage their rewards, payments, and account details. Every interaction required switching between separate systems with different interfaces, creating friction at exactly the moments where trust mattered most. LoyaltyCorp needed a white-labelled, client-adaptable solution that could sit inside any loyalty platform and handle real financial transactions securely.

What I designed

1
White-labelled consumer widget Designed a fully adaptable widget that client brands could customise to match their visual identity. The widget structure supported theming at the token level so each client deployment felt native to their platform without any redesign work.
2
Secure card and bank detail storage Designed the flows for adding, verifying, and managing saved payment methods, including nominal-amount card verification and secure storage UI that communicated trust clearly to users handling real financial data.
3
2FA and verification flows Designed two-factor authentication across card verification and account security, balancing compliance requirements with a low-friction experience. The flow needed to work reliably across varying network conditions and device types.
4
End-to-end payment flows Designed the full payment journey: P2P transfers, recurring payments, instalments, and bill pay. Each flow shared a consistent interaction pattern while accommodating the specific confirmation and error states each payment type required.
5
Client feature adaptability Worked with the product team to design a feature flag system at the UI level, so individual clients could enable or disable specific payment capabilities without visual inconsistency in the widget.
"Bridging the gap between brand loyalty and seamless transacting."

Key design decisions

Financial products live or die on trust, and trust is fragile in white-label contexts where users may not immediately recognise the brand behind the interface. Every interaction pattern was designed to feel deliberate and in control: no ambiguous states, clear confirmation at every step, and error messages that told users exactly what happened and what to do next.

The white-label constraint was treated as a strength rather than a limitation. By building the widget on a proper token system, each client could bring their brand identity without diverging from the interaction patterns users had already learned, keeping consistency where it counted most.
